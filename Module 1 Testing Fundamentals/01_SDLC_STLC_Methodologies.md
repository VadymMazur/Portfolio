# Lesson 1: SDLC & STLC. Software Development Methodologies

## 1. Comparative Analysis of Development Methodologies

I have analyzed the main software development methodologies, their pros and cons, and their suitability for different industries.

| # | Methodology | Strengths | Weaknesses | Target Industries |
|:--|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| 1 | **Waterfall** | Clear structure and sequential phases. Easy to plan budget and timelines. | Testing occurs at late stages; high cost of fixing early errors. | Government, Banking, Medical (regulated sectors) |
| 2 | **Incremental** | Working functionality is delivered early. Lower risk of total project failure. | Version management and integration can be complex. | Corporate systems (ERP, CRM) |
| 3 | **Scrum** | Fast response to changes. High team transparency and flexibility. | High dependency on the Product Owner's active involvement. | Startups, Product development, Web/Mobile |
| 4 | **Kanban** | Simple to implement. Visualizes workflow and eliminates bottlenecks. | Lack of clear iterations and long-term deadlines. | Support, DevOps, QA services |

### Methodology Deep Dive

* **Waterfall:** Ideal for large-scale projects with stable, fixed requirements where predictability is crucial. However, the "testing at the end" approach creates risks of not meeting user expectations.
* **Incremental:** Allows building the product piece by piece. Best for complex corporate systems that need to be launched in stages without stopping business processes.
* **Scrum:** Based on short sprints. It ensures the team doesn't waste time on outdated requirements but instead focuses on current market needs.
* **Kanban:** Perfect for continuous flows of tasks (like bug fixing or maintenance) where reaction speed is the top priority.

## 2. The Agile Manifesto: Origins and Impact

### Why did it appear?
In 2001, traditional approaches were too rigid and bureaucratic. The market demanded speed. The Agile Manifesto was created as a response to the crisis in traditional software engineering to provide flexibility, speed, and better communication.

### Challenges it aimed to solve:
1.  **Eliminating "Dilbert-style" Management:**
    * **Excessive Bureaucracy:** Rules and forms taking more time than actual coding.
    * **Disconnected Management:** Decisions made by leaders who don't understand the technical reality.
    * **Demotivation:** People working "for the process" rather than for the customer.
2.  **Agressive E-business Era:** The need to survive in a fast-paced digital economy.

### Has it succeeded?
Yes. Modern Agile implementation has led to:
* **Early testing and rapid feedback:** Every sprint generates a working product.
* **Client Collaboration:** Regular demos ensure the product matches the user's needs.
* **Team Autonomy:** Increased motivation through self-organization.

## 3. Practical Case: "Cat Photo Sharing" Startup

**Chosen Methodology:** `Scrum`

**Rationale:**
As the founder of a startup for a cat photo-sharing app, I would choose Scrum for several key reasons:
1.  **Dynamic Environment:** Startup requirements change daily. Scrum's sprint structure allows us to pivot or add new "purr-fect" features based on early user feedback.
2.  **Early MVP:** We can release a basic version (uploading a photo) in the first sprint and add filters or comments later.
3.  **Transparency:** Regular Stand-ups and Retrospectives keep the small startup team aligned and motivated.

Scrum provides the perfect balance of speed and flexibility, which is vital for surviving in the competitive mobile app market.
